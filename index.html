How I Build

My approach to shipping.

01 —
Functionality is the foundation
Before one animation, I nail the core problem. A clean interface can't save a weak system. But a strong system can survive a basic interface. No one remembers how it looked — they remember whether it worked.
02 —
Design before you generate
I broke the problem into smaller systems. Designed before generating. Iterated instead of accepting the first output. The difference isn't the tool — it's how you use it. Better thinking extracts better output from the same stack.
03 —
Ship during chaos, not after
College, labs, exams, hackathons — all overlapping. Time is not the issue. Priority is. I build in the gaps. Most people wait for the perfect window. That window never comes. You either build during chaos or you keep delaying.
04 —
Jump in first, learn fast
CTF with zero cybersecurity background — solved 15+ challenges anyway. POD store in 36 minutes. You learn faster when you're in it. The real advantage isn't knowledge — it's the willingness to look stupid until you're not.
